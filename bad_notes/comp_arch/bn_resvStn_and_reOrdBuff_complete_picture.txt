🔄 Putting It All Together: Flow of an Out-of-Order Instruction
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    1.  Fetch: CPU fetches multiple instructions per cycle.

    2.  Decode: Instruction is decoded, register operands are mapped via register renaming.

    3.  Issue:

          An available ROB entry is assigned.

          Instruction is placed in a Reservation Station.

          Operand readiness is checked.

    4.  Wait/Dispatch:

          RS tracks readiness.

          When ready, it dispatches to an execution unit.

    5.  Execute:

          Instruction is computed.

          Result is broadcast to waiting RSs and written to ROB.

    6.  Commit:

          Instruction at the ROB head is checked.

          If complete, the result is committed to the register file or memory.

          The ROB entry is retired.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
💡 Analogy
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Imagine a restaurant kitchen:

    Orders (Instructions) come in and are placed in a job queue (ROB).

    Chefs (Functional Units) look for orders they can start cooking — but only if all ingredients (operands) are available.

    Orders that are waiting for ingredients are held in reservation stations.

    Ingredients may come from earlier orders, and once they arrive, the chef starts cooking.

    The head waiter (commit logic) serves completed dishes in the order they were received, even if some dishes were cooked earlier.

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
🧠 Summary of Their Roles
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

    Reservation Stations: Dynamically schedule instruction execution based on operand availability, independent of original order.

    Reorder Buffer: Maintains program order for final updates, handles exceptions, and allows recovery from branch mispredictions.

Together, these structures form the backbone of modern dynamic scheduling CPUs (e.g., those following the Tomasulo algorithm), 
enabling high throughput and efficient use of CPU resources.
